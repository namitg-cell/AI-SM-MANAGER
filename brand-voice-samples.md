## LinkedIn Samples

### Sample 1
Did you know that in Washington State, sellers are legally required to fill out a Form 17 — a seller disclosure statement that reveals known issues with the property?

As a first-time buyer in Seattle, this is one of your most powerful tools. It covers everything from roof leaks to boundary disputes to environmental hazards.

But here's the thing most agents won't tell you: Form 17 only covers what the seller KNOWS about. That's why a home inspection is still non-negotiable.

If you're starting your home search in the Seattle area, understanding documents like these puts you in control of the process — not the other way around.

What questions do you have about the buying process? I'm here to help.

#SeattleRealEstate #FirstTimeHomeBuyer #HomeBuyingEducation

### Sample 2
The Seattle housing market in 2026 is showing some interesting trends for first-time buyers.

Median home prices in King County have stabilized compared to the rapid growth of 2021-2022. Interest rates are hovering around 6.5%, and inventory is slowly improving — especially in neighborhoods like Beacon Hill, Columbia City, and Rainier Beach.

What does this mean for you? More negotiating power than buyers have had in years.

Here are 3 things I'd recommend if you're thinking about buying this year:

1. Get pre-approved BEFORE you start touring homes
2. Look beyond the "hot" neighborhoods — value exists in emerging areas
3. Don't skip the inspection to "win" a bidding war — it's never worth it

The market rewards prepared buyers. Let's make sure that's you.

#SeattleHousing #FirstTimeHomeBuyer #RealEstateEducation
