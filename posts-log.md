## 2026-03-23 — Closing Costs for First-Time Buyers in Seattle

**Source:** Original topic: "closing costs for first-time buyers in Seattle"

### LinkedIn

A lot of first-time buyers in Seattle budget carefully for their down payment... then get blindsided by closing costs.

Here's the reality: closing costs in King County typically run 2-5% of your purchase price. On a median-priced Seattle home around $850K, that's roughly $17,000 to $42,500 on top of your down payment.

So what exactly are you paying for?

→ Loan origination fee (0.5-1% of the loan amount - this is your lender's processing fee)
→ Appraisal ($400-$700 in the Seattle area - confirms the home is worth what you're paying)
→ Title insurance (protects you if someone challenges ownership of the property)
→ Escrow fees (the neutral third party managing your transaction)
→ Prepaid property taxes (prorated from your closing date forward)
→ Homeowner's insurance (first year is typically due upfront)

Here's what most people miss: in Washington State, sellers can contribute toward your closing costs. Depending on your loan type, that's up to 3-6% of the sale price.

Your best move? Ask your lender for a Loan Estimate on day one. It itemizes every fee so nothing catches you off guard.
